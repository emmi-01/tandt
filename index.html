Manali

Manali is a popular tourist destination located in the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Here is a sample itinerary for a 3 night 4 day trip to Manali:

Day 1:

Arrive in Manali and check into your hotel or resort. Visit the Hadimba Temple, a popular Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Hadimba. Explore the Manali Mall Road, a popular shopping street with a variety of stores and restaurants.

Day 2:

Visit the Solang Valley, which is known for its adventure activities such as paragliding, zorbing, and skiing. Enjoy the picturesque views of snow-capped mountains and lush greenery. Return to Manali in the evening.

Day 3:

Take a day trip to Rohtang Pass, a high mountain pass located at an altitude of 3,978 meters. Enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding snow-capped mountains and glaciers. Return to Manali in the evening.

Day 4:

Visit the Naggar Castle, a historic castle built in the 15th century. Explore the Naggar Art Gallery, which houses a collection of traditional and contemporary art. Depart from Manali for your next destination. Note: This itinerary is just a sample and can be customized based on your interests and preferences. Also, please check the latest travel restrictions and guidelines before planning your trip to Manali.

Shimla

Shimla and Manali are two popular hill stations located in the northern part of India. Here is a suggested itinerary for a trip to Shimla and Manali:

Day 1: Arrive in Shimla

Check into your hotel and rest for a while. In the evening, take a walk along the Mall Road and explore the local markets.

Day 2: Shimla Sightseeing

Visit the Jakhu Temple, situated on a hilltop, offering panoramic views of Shimla. Next, visit the Vice Regal Lodge, a colonial-era building with beautiful architecture and gardens. Take a stroll at the Glen Forests and soak in the natural beauty of the place. In the evening, you can watch a movie at the Gaiety Theatre or attend a cultural event at the Ridge.

Day 3: Shimla to Manali

Check out of your hotel in Shimla and travel to Manali by road (around 7 hours). On the way, stop at the scenic Kullu Valley and visit the famous Kullu Shawl factories. Arrive in Manali and check into your hotel.

Day 4: Manali Sightseeing

Visit the Hadimba Temple, a unique temple dedicated to Hidimba Devi, located amidst dense deodar forests. Explore the Tibetan Monastery and the Van Vihar National Park. Take a dip in the hot water springs of Vashisht or indulge in adventure sports like paragliding or river rafting.

Day 5: Manali Adventure

Take an early morning drive to Solang Valley, where you can enjoy adventure sports like skiing, snowboarding, and zorbing. In the evening, you can take a stroll along the Mall Road and explore the local markets.

Day 6: Manali to Chandigarh

Check out of your hotel in Manali and travel to Chandigarh by road (around 8 hours). Check into your hotel in Chandigarh and rest for the day.

Day 7: Departure

On the last day of your trip, visit the Rock Garden, a beautiful garden made entirely of recycled materials. Afterward, visit the famous Sukhna Lake and take a boat ride. In the evening, depart for your next destination. Note: This itinerary is just a suggestion, and you can modify it based on your interests and preferences.

Spiti

Spiti Valley is a high altitude cold desert mountain valley located in the northern part of India, in the state of Himachal Pradesh. Here is a suggested itinerary for a Spiti tour:

Day 1: Arrival in Shimla

Arrive in Shimla and check into your hotel. Explore the local markets and the Mall Road.

Day 2: Shimla to Sarahan

Check out of your hotel in Shimla and travel to Sarahan by road (around 5 hours). Visit the Bhimakali Temple, a famous temple dedicated to Goddess Bhimakali. Check into your hotel and rest for the day.

Day 3: Sarahan to Sangla

Check out of your hotel in Sarahan and travel to Sangla by road (around 4 hours). Visit the Kamru Fort and the Sangla Valley. Check into your hotel and rest for the day.

Day 4: Sangla to Nako

Check out of your hotel in Sangla and travel to Nako by road (around 7 hours). Visit the Nako Lake, a beautiful lake surrounded by snow-capped mountains. Check into your hotel and rest for the day.

Day 5: Nako to Tabo

Check out of your hotel in Nako and travel to Tabo by road (around 3 hours). Visit the Tabo Monastery, a famous monastery known for its ancient murals and sculptures. Check into your hotel and rest for the day.

Day 6: Tabo to Kaza

Check out of your hotel in Tabo and travel to Kaza by road (around 3 hours). Visit the Key Monastery, a famous monastery located on a hilltop offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. Check into your hotel and rest for the day.

Day 7: Kaza Sightseeing

Visit the Kibber Village, one of the highest inhabited villages in the world. Next, visit the Langza Village and the Hikkim Village, known for their beautiful landscapes and ancient monasteries. In the evening, you can take a stroll along the Kaza Market and explore the local markets.

Day 8: Kaza to Chandratal Lake

Check out of your hotel in Kaza and travel to Chandratal Lake by road (around 6 hours). Enjoy the scenic beauty of the place and spend some time by the lake. Check into your campsite and rest for the day.

Day 9: Chandratal Lake to Manali

Check out of your campsite in Chandratal Lake and travel to Manali by road (around 7 hours). Check into your hotel in Manali and rest for the day.

Day 10: Departure

On the last day of your trip, check out of your hotel and depart for your next destination. Note: This itinerary is just a suggestion, and you can modify it based on your interests and preferences. Also, please note that due to the high altitude of the Spiti Valley, it is important to acclimatize yourself properly and take necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness.

Leh

Day 1: Arrival in Leh

Arrive in Leh and check into your hotel. Take it easy to acclimatize to the high altitude.

Day 2: Sightseeing in Leh

Visit the Shanti Stupa, a Buddhist white-domed stupa located on a hilltop with panoramic views of Leh. Next, visit the Leh Palace, a former royal palace overlooking the town. In the evening, explore the local markets and try some traditional Ladakhi cuisine.

Day 3: Leh to Nubra Valley

Check out of your hotel in Leh and travel to Nubra Valley by road (around 4 hours). Visit the Diskit Monastery, a 14th century monastery known for its statue of Maitreya Buddha. Take a camel ride in the sand dunes of Hunder. Check into your hotel or campsite in Nubra Valley and rest for the day.

Day 4: Nubra Valley to Pangong Lake

Check out of your hotel or campsite in Nubra Valley and travel to Pangong Lake by road (around 6 hours). Enjoy the scenic beauty of the place and spend some time by the lake. Check into your campsite and rest for the day.

Day 5: Pangong Lake to Leh

Check out of your campsite in Pangong Lake and travel back to Leh by road (around 6 hours). Visit the Hemis Monastery, a famous monastery known for its rich collection of ancient relics and manuscripts. Check into your hotel and rest for the day.

Day 6: Leh to Tso Moriri Lake

Check out of your hotel in Leh and travel to Tso Moriri Lake by road (around 7 hours). Enjoy the scenic beauty of the place and spend some time by the lake. Check into your campsite and rest for the day.

Day 7: Tso Moriri Lake to Leh

Check out of your campsite in Tso Moriri Lake and travel back to Leh by road (around 7 hours). Visit the Thiksey Monastery, a beautiful monastery known for its stunning architecture and scenic location. In the evening, you can take a stroll along the Leh Market and explore the local markets.

Day 8: Departure

On the last day of your trip, check out of your hotel and depart for your next destination. Note: This itinerary is just a suggestion, and you can modify it based on your interests and preferences. Also, please note that due to the high altitude of Ladakh, it is important to acclimatize yourself properly and take necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness.
